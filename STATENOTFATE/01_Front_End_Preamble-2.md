## Start here

If you have been depressed for a while, one of the hardest parts is that effort starts to feel pointless. That does not mean you are weak or uniquely broken. Depression can change energy, reward expectation, focus, timing, and the felt cost of action. Useful things can look too small to matter. Hard things can look permanent.

That matters because many people judge themselves from inside the state. They assume the hopeless interpretation is the accurate one. They assume failure to feel motivated means nothing can work. They assume a rough week proves the whole plan is fake. The front end is built to interrupt that reading error.

## What this project assumes

Depression is not only sadness. It can show up as exhaustion, numbness, shame, withdrawal, brain fog, irritability, or reward collapse.

Motivation is not a stable engine. Waiting to feel ready can keep people frozen for a very long time.

Function matters as much as mood. If daily life is shrinking, that matters even when feelings are hard to describe.

Small repeated corrections matter more than rare emotional surges.

## Why this now starts with hope and activation

Here, hope does not mean forced positivity. It means your brain's prediction of whether effort can still lead to improvement. When that prediction gets damaged, even useful suggestions can feel fake. So the first job is not to build a perfect routine. The first job is to produce one action small enough to survive resistance and one result visible enough to count as evidence.

## What happens next

The questionnaire identifies what depression looks like for you, what is getting hit hardest, and what kind of message you can still hear.

The intake process helps choose one starter lane instead of ten at once.

The opening plan is scaled to your real battery, not to an idealized version of you.

Early success is defined by continuity and proof, not by a dramatic mood transformation.
