# Canonical Project System

## Bottom line

This project treats depression as a systems failure that damages energy, reward prediction, follow-through, self-trust, perception, and ordinary function. The project is built for people who do not reliably have motivation, hope, consistency, or emotional buy-in. It assumes startup itself is a design problem.

## Core thesis

- Depression is not only sadness. It can look like exhaustion, numbness, shame, withdrawal, irritability, brain fog, reward collapse, drift, and loss of ordinary life range.
- People often judge themselves from inside the state. That creates reading errors: bad days look permanent, small actions look meaningless, and lapses look like proof the whole plan is fake.
- Because motivation is unstable, the system has to rely on structure, cues, friction reduction, visible proof, and state-based scaling.
- Function matters as much as mood. The project cares about wake time, hygiene, food, movement, environment, social contact, work capacity, and recovery from drift.
- Repetition matters more than intensity. A boring system that survives weak days beats a dramatic system that dies after two strong days.

## Front-end architecture

1. **Preamble** - Explain why the person feels stuck and why effort feels pointless.
2. **Hope and activation** - Treat hope as the brain's prediction that effort can still change something. If this signal is too damaged, later modules are unusable.
3. **Questionnaire** - Capture symptom burden, constraints, tone, and realistic battery level.
4. **Intake guide / personalization logic** - Convert answers into first focus, tone, and plan size.
5. **First operating plan** - Choose the smallest believable system that can actually run.

## Hope as gatekeeper

Hope here is not optimism. It is the working prediction that effort can still produce improvement.

Low-hope markers:
- small steps feel insulting
- the future feels closed
- every lapse gets read as proof of total failure
- useful suggestions are dismissed before testing

Hope is rebuilt by:
- small actions with visible traces
- fast feedback
- constrained choice instead of overwhelming choice
- written proof that a bad day no longer erases the week
- repeating before escalating

Gatekeeper rule: do not move deeper into the program just because the person understands it intellectually. Move only after there is at least one believable action -> outcome link.

## Startup logic

The startup sequence is:
1. name why action feels pointless
2. offer a short menu of starter lanes
3. choose one believable action
4. attach it to a cue
5. record before/after
6. repeat before expanding

Starter lanes already defined in the project:
- body
- room
- routine
- people
- treatment

## Daily operating system

The project implicitly runs three layers:

- **Level 1 / survival** - wake, water, meds if prescribed, food, light, hygiene minimums
- **Level 2 / stabilization** - movement, one task block, one reset, one non-passive act
- **Level 3 / expansion** - social reconnection, projects, exercise progression, ambition, meaning-building

It also assumes state-based execution:
- low day
- medium day
- strong day
- collapse day

The plan has to change with the day type instead of forcing one standard.

## Core modules named in the source base

- sleep / circadian regularity / light
- movement / behavioral activation
- food / hydration / medication consistency
- hygiene / environment
- rumination / shame / self-attack interruption
- social reintegration and contact protection
- substance and coping reality
- therapy / medication / formal treatment integration

## What counts as early success

Early success is not dramatic mood change. It is:
- lower chaos
- fewer zero-days
- better mornings
- more continuity
- shorter recovery after bad hits
- visible proof that tomorrow can still be shaped
- growing self-trust based on evidence, not hype
