# Canonical Five-Year Model

## General logic

The long-horizon model exists to stop two common errors:
- expecting quick emotional transformation from early recovery work
- using the same job description for every stage of recovery

The plan divides recovery into stages so each period has the right job.

## Stage summary

- **Year 1 - Stabilization and de-chaos**  
  Job: stop the floor from moving.  
  Main wins: wake time, light, food, hydration, hygiene, movement, environment, written structure, fewer zero-days.

- **Year 2 - Capacity building**  
  Job: turn fragile stability into usable capacity.  
  Main wins: better stamina, more reliable task completion, stronger exercise and sleep protection, less overshoot-crash.

- **Year 3 - Reintegration**  
  Job: reconnect with interest, people, identity, and ordinary participation.  
  Main wins: more initiative, more social tolerance, wider life range, return of selected pleasure and meaning.

- **Year 4 - Expansion without self-sabotage**  
  Job: pursue bigger goals without outrunning the nervous system.  
  Main wins: more ambition without collapse, better standards without brittle overreach.

- **Year 5 - Consolidation and selective ambition**  
  Job: keep what actually supports life and cut ornamental or draining effort.  
  Main wins: durability, precision, self-trust, non-negotiables, cleaner tradeoff awareness.

- **Maintenance**  
  Job: protect gains, detect drift early, tighten fast, avoid waiting for collapse to provide clarity.

## Core Year 1 message

Year 1 is structural, not cinematic. The project repeatedly states that early wins are boring on purpose:
- regular wake window
- morning light
- water and food
- meds if prescribed
- minimum viable movement
- short environmental resets
- brief meditation or breathing as interruption tools
- daily cards and weekly reviews

Success in Year 1 means fewer structureless days and faster restart after setbacks, not "feeling cured."

## Stage risks

Each stage has its own main failure mode:
- Year 1 -> trying to transform too much too fast
- Year 2 -> overscheduling when energy improves
- Year 3 -> mistaking partial recovery for full recovery
- Year 4 -> letting ambition outrun anchors
- Year 5 -> dropping basics because life feels better
- Maintenance -> ignoring subtle drift

## Measurement themes in the long-horizon source

The five-year document emphasizes:
- wake-time adherence
- light exposure
- medication adherence if relevant
- movement days
- zero-day count
- collapse recovery time
- task completion ratio
- overshoot-crash tracking
- environment order
- mood / function trend
- self-trust score
- relapse and drift mapping

## Practical use

For AI ingestion, this file is the long-horizon logic source. It tells the model what job belongs to each stage, what success looks like at that stage, and what kind of failure to watch for.
