# Depression Project Architecture Parsed

## Main thesis

The depression project treats depression as a systems failure affecting:

- energy
- timing
- reward prediction
- follow-through
- self-trust
- function
- environment interaction

It argues that purely intellectual insight often fails because the illness also damages startup, not just belief.

## Core design principles

- Start with function, not abstract mood talk alone.
- Treat motivation as unstable.
- Use leverage, not volume.
- Prefer repetition over drama.
- Scale the plan to actual battery level.
- Separate state from identity.

## Front-end logic

The front end is sequenced because order matters:

1. explain why stuckness and effort-pointlessness happen
2. restore usable hope
3. capture burden, constraints, and tone
4. choose the smallest believable operating plan

## Hope logic

Hope is framed as the prediction that effort can still change outcome.

When hope is damaged:

- good advice sounds fake
- plans feel insulting or decorative
- the user may refuse correct tools behaviorally even if they understand them intellectually

Therefore the early system must generate:

- believable
- small
- visible
- repeatable proof

## Core anchor set

The repeated anchor base across the corpus is:

- wake time regularity
- morning light
- hydration
- basic meal timing
- medication adherence if relevant
- minimum viable movement
- hygiene continuity
- environment reset

## Daily execution hierarchy

### Level 1: survival

- wake
- water
- meds if prescribed
- food
- light
- hygiene minimums

### Level 2: stabilization

- movement
- one task block
- one reset
- one non-passive act

### Level 3: expansion

- longer work
- deliberate exercise
- richer contact
- chosen leisure and larger goals

## Five-year sequence

- Launch: baseline, friction reduction, visibility
- Year 1: stabilization and de-chaos
- Year 2: capacity building
- Year 3: reintegration
- Year 4: expansion without self-sabotage
- Year 5: consolidation and durable fit
- Maintenance: earlier correction and relapse prevention

## Strategic lesson

The project repeatedly insists that early-stage systems must not be forced to carry late-stage ambitions.

That is one of the most important reusable ideas in the entire corpus.
