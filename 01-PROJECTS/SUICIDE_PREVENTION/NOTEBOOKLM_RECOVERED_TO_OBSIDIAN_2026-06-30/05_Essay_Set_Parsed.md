# Essay Set Parsed

## 1. Suicide is best understood as a systems problem

The current corpus does not support framing suicide as a simple moral failure or a single-cause event. The strongest sources converge on a systems view: risk stacks, protective factors, social conditions, care access, support continuity, and communication practices all shape the landscape in which suicidal crises happen.

## 2. Depression damages startup before it damages grand theory

One of the most important project insights is that depressed people often do not primarily need better abstract advice. They need lower startup cost. The system must help them move before they feel ready.

## 3. Hope is not hype

Hope is best treated as the prediction that effort can still change outcome. When that prediction collapses, even good advice sounds false. The answer is proof, not speeches.

## 4. Anchors are infrastructure

Wake time, light, hydration, food timing, movement, hygiene, and room reset keep appearing because they are infrastructure, not decorations. They survive weak days better than inspiration does.

## 5. Restart fidelity beats perfect compliance

The corpus repeatedly rejects shame-based perfection. Misses happen. The system should be designed so a bad day does not become a lost week.

## 6. Suicide prevention belongs inside a broader recovery architecture

Crisis resources matter, but they work better when they sit inside a wider system that addresses isolation, chaos, narrowing, environmental drag, and the disappearance of ordinary support structures.

## 7. Anti-Gravity works best as an adjunct

The best version of Anti-Gravity is bounded. It is useful because it does not pretend to be more than it is. It helps the user act, route, and restart without pretending to become a clinician.

## 8. State not fate is an agency claim without fantasy

The deepest project claim is not that suffering is easy to overcome. It is that a current state is not final identity. The project tries to restore agency without lying about the difficulty.
