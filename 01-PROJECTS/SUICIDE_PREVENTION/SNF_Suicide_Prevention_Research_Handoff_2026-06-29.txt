## 3. Handoff For The New Suicide Prevention And Education Tabs In SNF

Primary build goal:
Make the suicide-prevention area feel like a serious educational appendix and supporter-preparation layer, not a lightweight crisis widget and not a fake clinical tool.

What the tabs should do:

1. Get Safe Now
- Keep 988 and 911 context visible.
- Use low-friction language: reduce danger for the next hour, contact a human, do not solve life while flooded.
- Keep personal contacts and calm scripts easy to reach.

2. Warning Signs And Risk
- Separate warning signs, acute danger indicators, background risks, and protection stack.
- Show behavior changes, agitation, hopelessness, isolation, substance use, shame, sleep disruption, and care dropout in plain language.
- Avoid score-like language.

3. Support Plan And Reflection
- Keep this collaborative and practical.
- Include scripts for asking for help, helping someone else, and preparing for a care visit.
- Build around a support map: anchor person, buffer contact, recurring environment.

4. Clinical And Community Guide
- Keep this as an educational map, not treatment instructions.
- Explain public health, clinical care, community trust, care transitions, population-specific considerations, and data infrastructure.
- Link outward to official sources and toolkits.

5. Evidence / Sources
- Tag every major source by type and disposition.
- Use categories: official anchor, implementation tool, research anchor, project synthesis, watchlist.
- Show why each source matters and how it should or should not influence public claims.

6. Population-aware lanes to keep building
- Perinatal / postpartum
- Youth and school
- Veterans
- LGBTQ+
- AI/AN and tribal/community grounded sources
- Older adult / rural / disability
- Black communities and racism-shaped stress
- Latino / immigrant / refugee communities
- Work / unemployment / financial-strain pathways

7. Missing content still worth building
- Measurement section for appendix engagement and help-seeking without risk scoring.
Current status: supporter script library, postvention section, and safer messaging section are now present in the public appendix; future work should deepen them rather than treating them as missing.
- Visual quick maps and text-first infographic-style aids are now present in the public appendix; future work should deepen them with more settings-specific and population-specific variants.
- Community support paths and helper compare blocks are now present in the public appendix; future work should deepen them with more source-backed routing examples and population-specific nuances.
- An evidence glossary for route cards is now present in the public appendix so the page explains why direct questions, short route cards, context-aware framing, and no-risk-score rules are used.
- Setting playbooks are now present in the public appendix so home, work/school, community/faith, and health/benefits settings each have a first move, common mistake, escalation cue, route cards, and source footing.
- Population-aware prevention tabs now carry route cards and source footing, so they function more like mini-modules than short summaries.

8. Product boundaries to keep visible
- This is research and education support, not crisis counseling or medical advice.
- Do not ask for graphic detail.
- Do not imply the app can determine true suicide risk.
- Do not present journaling or self-reflection as sufficient in acute danger.
