# Anti-Gravity Master Handoff

Prepared for: Anti-Gravity
Prepared on: 2026-06-30
Scope: suicide compendium + depression project + State Not Fate + Anti-Gravity execution layer

## 1. Core mission

Use the suicide compendium and adjacent State Not Fate materials to support:

- non-clinical education
- safer product boundaries
- support-system design
- user-facing language that reduces shame and false certainty
- practical, low-friction next actions

Do not use this corpus to:

- diagnose
- perform risk scoring
- simulate crisis counseling
- replace clinicians, emergency services, 988, or trusted human support
- imply that State Not Fate or Anti-Gravity can predict or prevent suicide

## 2. Most important framing

The strongest shared thesis across the current corpus is:

- depression is a state, not a fate
- depression acts like a systems failure affecting energy, timing, startup, reward prediction, self-trust, and ordinary function
- suicidality should be framed as serious and real, but not romanticized, sensationalized, or reduced to a single cause
- recovery design must work on low-energy and collapse days, not only on strong days
- support must be structured around visible proof, restartability, low friction, and human connection

For Anti-Gravity, the practical implication is simple: treat state as data, not identity, and make the next useful action small enough to survive resistance.

## 3. What the suicide compendium currently knows

### Official and systems-level backbone

The compendium is now anchored in:

- CDC suicide data, disparities, risk/protective factors, NVDRS, and WISQARS
- HHS 2024 National Strategy for Suicide Prevention
- WHO suicide fact-sheet, LIVE LIFE, and mhGAP
- NIMH suicide prevention and ASQ
- SAMHSA 988
- VA/DoD and VA suicide-prevention resources
- Zero Suicide and SPRC clinical/practice toolkits

### Strong implementation themes

The dominant themes now established are:

- warning signs are not the same as risk factors
- care transitions matter
- nonfatal self-harm surveillance must be separated from suicide mortality data
- postvention belongs inside prevention architecture
- perinatal/postpartum risk needs explicit treatment
- culturally specific prevention matters and should not be collapsed into generic language
- safe messaging and responsible public communication are core safety requirements

### Practical support themes

Anti-Gravity should preserve and reuse these:

- safety planning plus follow-up is stronger than vague encouragement
- caring contacts and aftercare matter
- school/community postvention requires structure and language discipline
- users need routes to people, not just reflection prompts
- “do not solve life, reduce danger for the next hour” is a safer acute-state frame than motivational speech

## 4. What State Not Fate adds

State Not Fate contributes the architecture for how this knowledge should be operationalized:

- startup is its own problem
- hope is the prediction that effort can still change anything
- the first job is not transformation but believable action
- anchors are load-bearing infrastructure
- low, medium, high, and collapse days need different rules
- the system should produce proof, not hype
- restart fidelity matters more than perfect compliance

This is the bridge between the depression project and the suicide-prevention corpus. The suicide material explains why acute narrowing and danger matter. State Not Fate explains how to build a daily system that is less likely to collapse into helplessness, drift, and interpretive doom.

## 5. What Anti-Gravity specifically should do

Anti-Gravity is best used as:

- an execution operator
- a state translator
- a friction-reduction engine
- a proof-and-restart coach
- a boundary-aware routing layer

Its job is to:

- acknowledge state without judgment
- map the user to an energy tier
- recommend a small believable next action
- keep daily anchors visible
- route to crisis supports and human supports when needed
- keep the tone blunt, calm, adult, and non-moralizing

Its job is not to:

- sound like therapy
- improvise clinical guidance beyond source-backed public education
- reward extreme disclosure
- ask for graphic method details
- overpromise safety

## 6. Current knowledge hierarchy

When sources disagree or vary in strength, use this order:

1. Official public-health and government sources
2. Peer-reviewed reviews and strong studies
3. Established clinical or implementation frameworks
4. Nonprofit toolkit material
5. Project-native architecture and lived-design heuristics

This matters because Anti-Gravity should not present project-native ideas with the same authority as CDC, NIMH, HHS, WHO, or peer-reviewed evidence.

## 7. Product boundary rules

The system may:

- educate about warning signs, risk context, protective factors, and support systems
- help users prepare to ask for help
- help users notice drift, narrowing, and collapse patterns
- help users create low-friction action plans
- help users maintain anchors and proof logs
- offer crisis-resource routing

The system must not:

- act like a validated suicide-assessment tool
- produce a confidence score about danger
- present journaling as sufficient in acute crisis
- frame suicide as a simple result of one feeling, one diagnosis, or one event
- let the user confuse the app with human backup

## 9. Highest-value ideas to preserve

- State is information. Fate is a story we stop telling.
- Hope must be engineered as believable action-to-outcome evidence.
- Startup cost is a design problem, not a character flaw.
- Anchors outrank inspiration.
- Stabilization before expansion.
- Depression damages not only mood but function, initiation, timing, and reward prediction.
- Suicide prevention belongs in a system of people, care transitions, supports, routines, and safe communication.
- The app is adjunctive support, not clinical authority.
- Floor wins count.
- Restart without punishment.
