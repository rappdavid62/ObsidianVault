# Depression Project - Grok Master Brief

## What this project is
This is a long-horizon depression recovery framework built around realistic execution under low energy, inconsistent follow-through, emotional volatility, shame, avoidance, and environmental friction.

It is not framed as inspiration, mindset, or generic wellness. It treats depression as a systems problem touching perception, reward prediction, energy, sleep, behavior, environment, and social functioning.

## Core thesis
Depression persists partly because it distorts valuation, timing, self-appraisal, and reward expectation. People then behave in ways that accidentally train the state further: avoidance, irregular sleep, underfeeding, low light, isolation, self-attack, inactivity, room decay, and inconsistent treatment adherence.

The project tries to interrupt that loop by building anchors and sequences that still work on weak days.

## Main ideas that recur across files
- depression is broader than sadness
- function matters as much as mood
- motivation is unreliable
- repetition matters more than intensity
- anchors should be linked into a small system
- low / medium / high / collapse days need different rules
- hope should be treated as the brain's prediction that effort can still lead to improvement
- recovery is staged: stabilization -> capacity -> reintegration -> expansion -> consolidation -> maintenance

## What the front end is trying to do
The front end should make a depressed person understand:
- why they feel stuck
- why effort currently feels pointless
- why waiting to feel motivated is a trap
- why small repeated actions rebuild effort -> outcome credibility
- why hope is a system signal, not just a mood word

## Preferred tone
Blunt, intelligent, practical, non-patronizing, psychologically realistic, and concrete.
Avoid generic inspiration language, vague positivity, and overclinical flattening.
