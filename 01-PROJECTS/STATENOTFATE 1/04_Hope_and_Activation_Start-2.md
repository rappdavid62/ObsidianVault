```text
TABLE
Definition used here Hope is the brain's prediction of whether effort will lead to improvement. In this project it is treated as a system signal, not a mood label.
```

Why this comes before the rest

A person can understand the program and still not use it because their system predicts that effort will fail. Once that prediction is strong enough, good advice starts sounding insulting, fake, or too small to matter. That is why hope and activation sit in front of the rest of the plan.

What low hope looks like

Small steps feel insulting.

The future feels closed or already decided.

Every lapse is interpreted as proof of total failure.

Even useful suggestions are rejected because expected payoff feels near zero.

What rebuilds hope

Small actions with visible results

Fast feedback

Constrained choice instead of overwhelming freedom

Written proof that bad days no longer erase the whole week

Repeated interruption of the 'nothing works' interpretation

Startup sequence

Name why action feels pointless.

Offer a short menu of starter lanes.

Choose one action that feels believable.

Attach it to a cue.

Record before and after.

Repeat before expanding.

Starter lanes

```text
TABLE
Lane | First-action examples
Body | outside for 3 minutes; stretch for 2 songs; water + meds if prescribed
Room | one bag of trash; clear one surface; 10-minute reset
Routine | wake within a window; lights out cue; morning card
People | one text; one reply; one scheduled check-in
Treatment | book visit; refill meds; read one treatment note
```

When not to move on yet

The person still treats every suggestion as fake or pointless.

The first action keeps being dismissed before it is attempted.

The plan keeps overshooting and confirming futility.

There is still no believable action -> outcome link in the person's mind.
